Spaghetti

There is nothing better than a nice bowl of spaghetti with a hearty meat sauce. In this recipe you will learn how to make it for yoursef! You will be surprised just how simple it can be and with a little preperation, you won't have a thing to worry about.

As always BE PREPARED!! There are several steps in theis recipe. Having your ingredients ready and prepped for use will make this process much smoother and allow you to time everything perfectly.

Ingredients

  • 1lb pasta (You can really use any kind of noodles you want!)
  • 2 cans crushed red tomatoes
  • 3-4 strips of thick cut bacon (small dice)
  • 5 cloves of garlic (finely diced)
  • 1/2 large white onion (diced)
  • italian seasoning (to taste)
  • 2 tsp red pepper flakes
  • large pinch of sugar
  • salt and pepper (to taste)

Recipe

  1. Step 1: Place diced bacon in cold pan and turn stove to medium-high.
  2. Step 2: When bacon has started to crisp, remove it but leave the rendered bacon fat. Reduce heat to medium
  3. Step 3: Toss in your onions and saute until onions have begun to carmalize.
  4. Step 4: Add your garlic and saute until fragrant. Usually only a minute or so.
  5. Step 5: Pour in the crushed tomatoes. Add a splash of water to the cans and swish it around to get the excess out and pour that in as well.
  6. Step 6: Return the bacon you cooked to the sauce.
  7. Step 7: Now you will add the italian seasoning, red pepper flakes, sugar, salt, and pepper.
  8. Step 8: Cover your sauce, leaving a small opening to vent. Let simmer for 30 minutes.
  9. Step 9: When there is about 10 minutes left on your sauce timer, bring a pot of water to a boil. Salt generously.
  10. Step 10: Once boiling, add your pasta to the water. Follow cooking directions of your preffered pasta.
  11. Step 11: When the pasta is al dente, strain the water but reserve a little of the pasta water. (You can use this if your noodles stick together when they get dry)
  12. Step 12: Place a serving of noodles on plate and top with sauce.
  13. Step 13: Enjoy!
